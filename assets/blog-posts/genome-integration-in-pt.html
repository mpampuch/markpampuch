Improving Nuclear Genome editing in Phaeodactylum tricornutum

A sizable portion of my master's research involved developping methodologies for improving integrattion of transgenic DNA in the marine diatom Phaeodactylum tricornutum in the pursuit of create stable genomic landing pads.

As a breif overview of the project, engineering photosynthetic microbes has been a proposed method for sustainably producing natural resources without causing further harm to our planet. One candidate microbe for this purpose is called Phaeodactylum tricornutum. By engineering this microbe, researchers have been able to use it to produce chemicals involved in creating plastics, pharmaceuticals, and even COVID-19 diagnostic tests. Many genetic tools have been developed for this microbe to make engineering easier, however one of the bottlenecks that has limitted this microorganism's potential for industrial use was the lack of reliable methods for integrating transgenes into its nuclear genome.

I have been able to establish a simple, efficient, and reliable protocol to introduce custom DNA into this organism's genome. I've also analyzed the method of integration and stability of the transgenes in the genome. This work lays the groundwork for developing reliable insertion sites and targeted nuclear genome engineering techniques in this promising microorganism.
